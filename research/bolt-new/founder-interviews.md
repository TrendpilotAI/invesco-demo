# Bolt.new — Founder Interviews & Key Quotes

## Eric Simons — CEO & Founder

### Background
- Started programming at 13 in Naperville, Illinois
- Built ClassConnect for a high school chemistry teacher who wanted more engaging classes
- Famously lived secretly at AOL's Palo Alto HQ for 2 months in 2011 (age 19)
  - Accessed through Imagine K12 education incubator
  - After program ended, kept using his badge
  - Slept on couches, used gym, showers, food
  - Discovered by security guard — became Silicon Valley legend
- Serial entrepreneur: ClassConnect → Thinkster (acquired Dec 2018) → StackBlitz/Bolt.new

### On the Near-Death Experience
> "We had stalled out at $0.7M ARR and were weeks away from shutting down."

- December 2023 board meeting: investors gave ultimatum — prove traction in 2024 or close
- Eric and Albert Pai considered selling the company
- Had built WebContainers for 7 years with no repeatable revenue model

### On the Claude 3.5 Sonnet Moment
> "We had exactly the right technology to unlock the opportunity. Suddenly we arrived at a moment when a data center can magically stream code in a browser, and we had the tech to run it."

> "There's an order of magnitude difference in the LLM's required infrastructure to make it functional versus zero shot. Claude 3.5 Sonnet is the enabling technology that made this product possible, period."

- Feb 2024: Built early prototype — models weren't good enough
- June 2024: Got access to Claude 3.5 Sonnet — "agentic coding skills were leaps-and-bounds better"
- July 1, 2024: Hard-pivoted back to the prototype
- 90-day build sprint → October 3 launch

### On the Pivot Philosophy
> "Software doesn't have to be built only by traditional developers and teams. It can now be prompted into existence by the end users themselves, for themselves."

- Inspiration from Canva: "Canva enabled non-designers to create. What if we could do that for software?"
- Before AI, only developers could build web apps — and they didn't want browser-based IDEs
- Removing the IDE entirely was the key insight

### On WebContainers as the Moat
> "For seven years we kept grinding even when the results looked thin, and we were disciplined enough not to run out of money. This persistence meant that when LLMs finally became good enough to generate entire apps, we were holding the exact missing piece."
> — Albert Pai, CTO

### On Growth
> "Zero to $4M ARR in 4 weeks" — Eric Simons, tweeted November 2024

- Company went from near-shutdown to breakout growth in weeks
- "One of the fastest-growing AI tools globally"
- Reached $40M ARR in 5 months with <40 employees

### On Retention Challenges
- Acknowledged high churn in AI coding market
- Pivoted strategy to focus on retention through bundled services
- Updated subscription model to include hosting, databases, auth, domains
- Goal: "retentive business" beyond just AI inference

### On Company Culture
- Daily all-hands calls to squash bugs and push features
- Hired community staff to engage users and gather feedback
- Plans to have more AI agents than human employees
- <10 GTM staff for a $40M ARR business — extremely lean

### On the Anthropic Relationship
- Anthropic offered $300K unlimited WebContainer license in spring 2024
- Deal expired unsigned — StackBlitz kept full control
- Claude API sits at core of Bolt
- Anthropic published official case study on StackBlitz/Bolt

## Albert Pai — CTO & Co-founder

### On WebContainers
> "People assume we're running a giant server farm. In reality, the server is your browser."

> "At the beginning, we weren't even sure it was possible. Browsers don't expose the syscalls Node expects and have strict security rules. We ended up spending years porting and building our own pieces until it actually felt like a local dev environment."

### On Persistence
> "For seven years we kept grinding even when the results looked thin, and we were disciplined enough not to run out of money."

## Dominic Elm — Founding Engineer

### On WebContainer Performance
> "The big win is latency. It feels as if you're developing on localhost. Plus it has security benefits. With remote compute you get abuse like people trying to run bitcoin miners. With WebContainer, if someone tries, they're just using their own CPU."
