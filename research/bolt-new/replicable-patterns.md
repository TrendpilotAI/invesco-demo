# Bolt.new — Replicable Patterns

## What We Can Copy/Adapt

### 1. The "Magic Moment" Launch
**Pattern:** Launch with zero friction and an instant "wow" — no signup, no setup, immediate value.

**How Bolt did it:**
- No login required to start
- Type a prompt → see a working app in <60 seconds
- Single tweet launch, no ads, no press release
- Let the product speak for itself

**How to replicate:**
- Design for the fastest possible time-to-value
- Remove every barrier between "curious" and "amazed"
- The first 60 seconds IS your marketing
- If your product needs explanation, it's not ready

### 2. Usage-Based Pricing with Generous Free Tier
**Pattern:** Give enough free value to create genuine "aha moments," then charge based on consumption.

**How Bolt did it:**
- 1M tokens/month free (enough to build small projects)
- Token-based tiers from $25–$2,000/month
- Started with $9/mo unlimited → quickly pivoted when users burned through limits

**How to replicate:**
- Free tier must deliver real value, not a teaser
- Usage-based pricing aligns cost with value received
- Be willing to pivot pricing fast (Bolt did it in days)
- Low marginal cost (browser-side compute) enables generous free tier

### 3. Technology Moat + Market Timing
**Pattern:** Years of foundational tech work meets sudden market readiness.

**How Bolt did it:**
- 7 years of WebContainers development
- When Claude 3.5 Sonnet arrived, they had "the exact missing piece"
- 90-day sprint from pivot decision to launch

**How to replicate:**
- Build deep technical moats, even when revenue lags
- Stay alive long enough for the market to catch up
- When the window opens, move FAST (90 days, not 9 months)
- The moat isn't the AI — it's the infrastructure the AI runs on

### 4. Integration as Distribution
**Pattern:** Plug into tools your users already use — their ecosystem becomes your distribution.

**How Bolt did it:**
- Day 1: Netlify integration (1M+ developer audience)
- Supabase integration (pulled in indie hackers, product managers)
- Expo integration (opened entire React Native mobile dev segment)
- GitHub, Stripe integrations expanded use cases

**How to replicate:**
- Identify 3-5 platforms your target users already live on
- Build integrations that are genuinely useful, not just checkboxes
- Each integration = new user segment + co-marketing opportunity
- Partners promote you because you drive them traffic/usage

### 5. Founder-Led "Build in Public" Marketing
**Pattern:** CEO publicly shares metrics, struggles, and wins — creates authentic narrative.

**How Bolt did it:**
- Eric Simons tweeted "$0 to $4M ARR in 30 days"
- Investors amplified on LinkedIn
- Podcast circuit for deeper storytelling
- Transparent about near-death experience → comeback narrative

**How to replicate:**
- Share real numbers (ARR, users, growth rate)
- The "nearly died, then exploded" narrative is catnip for tech media
- Podcast appearances scale founder's story
- Investor amplification multiplies reach

### 6. User-Generated Content as Growth Engine
**Pattern:** Make your product's output inherently shareable.

**How Bolt did it:**
- People naturally share "look what I built" on social media
- LinkedIn posts: "Built our MVP in a weekend on Bolt.new" → thousands of views
- YouTube tutorials created organically by community (1,000+ videos)
- Reddit community (r/boltnewbuilders) = organic support + marketing

**How to replicate:**
- Design outputs people want to show off
- Make sharing frictionless (one-click deploy helps)
- Highlight/amplify user content (retweets, showcases)
- Create showcase channels where users can flex

### 7. Hackathon Strategy for Community & Content
**Pattern:** Large-scale hackathons generate community, content, and PR simultaneously.

**How Bolt did it:**
- Started with $3K challenges with influencers
- Scaled to "World's Largest Hackathon": 130K participants, $1M+ prizes
- Partner challenge tracks (Algorand = $25K) = co-funded prizes
- Pieter Levels as judge = indie hacker credibility
- Winners featured on blog = ongoing content

**How to replicate:**
- Start small (micro-challenges with influencers)
- Scale with partner co-funding
- Celebrity judges drive participation
- Document everything = months of content
- Use hackathon to discover power users and advocates

### 8. The .new Domain Hack
**Pattern:** Domain name itself is a call-to-action.

**How Bolt did it:**
- bolt.new implies "create something new now"
- Memorable, shareable, one-word (+ TLD)
- Google .new TLD associated with quick creation actions

**How to replicate:**
- Domain name matters more than people think
- Action-oriented domains drive clicks and recall
- Consider .new, .ai, .app, .dev TLDs strategically

### 9. Lean GTM with Product-Led Growth
**Pattern:** <10 GTM staff for $40M ARR — let the product sell itself.

**How Bolt did it:**
- <40 total employees, <10 in GTM
- No outbound sales team (until enterprise)
- Community team for engagement, not sales
- "Bolt Builders" freelancer network extends support without headcount

**How to replicate:**
- If your product needs a sales team to sell, improve the product
- Community managers > SDRs for PLG
- Freelancer/contractor networks scale support cheaply
- Invest in product quality, not marketing spend

### 10. Rapid Pricing Iteration
**Pattern:** Launch pricing, observe behavior, pivot in days, not quarters.

**How Bolt did it:**
- $9/mo unlimited → users burned through in 48 hours
- Pivoted to token-based within days
- Result: ~50% of users upgraded to higher tiers
- Revenue went from $60K to millions per month

**How to replicate:**
- Don't overthink initial pricing — ship and observe
- Watch what users actually DO, not what they say
- Be willing to change pricing radically and quickly
- Usage-based aligns incentives: users pay when they get value

## Meta-Pattern: The Compound Advantage

The real lesson isn't any single tactic — it's the compound effect:
1. **Deep tech moat** (7 years of WebContainers)
2. **Perfect timing** (Claude 3.5 Sonnet release)
3. **Zero-friction product** (no signup, instant value)
4. **Viral by design** (shareable outputs)
5. **Community amplification** (users become marketers)
6. **Integration distribution** (partners = channels)
7. **Lean execution** (<40 people)

Each element amplifies the others. The moat enables the free tier. The free tier drives adoption. Adoption creates content. Content drives more adoption. Integrations expand the addressable market. Lean team means high margins despite AI costs.

**The uncomfortable truth:** Most of this only works because the product is genuinely 10x better than alternatives for its use case. Marketing tactics without product-market fit are just noise.
