# Poolside AI — Founder Interviews & Key Quotes

## Jason Warner — President & Co-Founder

### Background
- **Former CTO of GitHub** — oversaw the platform used by 100M+ developers
- Previously SVP of Technology at Canonical (Ubuntu)
- Deep understanding of developer tools, enterprise sales, open source ecosystem

### Key Quotes & Positions

**On AGI & Software**
> "The fastest path to AGI runs through software engineering... Software development requires understanding the world, the ability to do multistep complex reasoning, and the ability to plan across long-horizon objectives."

**On Intelligence Timeline**
> "Intelligence on compute [is] just 36 months away" (claimed AI agents will achieve human-level software development within 3 years)

**On Foundation Models**
> "Most companies should NOT build their own foundation models" — provocative positioning that only a few companies (like Poolside) should build models, while everyone else should use theirs

**On Target Customers**
> "We sell to government defense, very large FSIS banks... legacy tech, enterprise tech folks" — explicitly focused on organizations with 5,000+ developers

**On Developer Identity**
> Discussed "A Developer Identity Crisis" — how AI changes what it means to be a software engineer, the skills developers need to evolve

**On the Market**
> Called AI "alien intelligence" — framing it as fundamentally different from human intelligence, not just automation

### Interview Themes
- Consistently frames Poolside's mission in AGI terms, not just "coding assistant"
- Positions against both cloud-only AI companies AND lightweight tool makers
- Emphasizes enterprise security as existential for AI adoption
- Uses GitHub experience as credibility anchor
- Makes bold timeline predictions to generate buzz

## Eiso Kant — CTO & Co-Founder

### Background
- **Founded source{d}** — applied AI/ML to source code analysis
- Deep technical expertise in code-specific AI
- European entrepreneur with strong technical community ties

### Key Quotes & Positions

**On Technical Architecture**
> Revealed the "Model Factory" concept — a system for continuous model improvement through RLCEF, synthetic data generation, and automated evaluation

**On Training Approach**
> Models learn by "writing and executing code in a sandbox environment of over 10,000 real-world codebases" — emphasizing learning from both success AND failure

**On Project Horizon**
> "Project Horizon represents a crucial step for the company to own not just the intelligence layer, but the energy and compute foundation that supports it"

### Interview Themes
- More technically detailed than Warner — discusses model architecture, training methodology
- Focuses on the "Model Factory" as Poolside's competitive advantage
- Discusses infrastructure (compute, data centers) as strategic assets
- Less public-facing than Warner but appears in technical interviews

## Messaging Framework (Extracted from Interviews)

### Core Narrative
"We're building AGI, starting with software. Software is the hardest cognitive task, and solving it unlocks everything else. We deploy inside your security boundary because the most important work happens with the most sensitive data."

### Key Talking Points (Repeated Across Interviews)
1. AGI through code (not chatbots or image generation)
2. Security-first / on-premises / data sovereignty
3. Most companies shouldn't build models — use ours
4. 3 years to human-level AI coding agents
5. We don't hand off and walk away — FDREs ensure outcomes
6. Intelligence ON compute, not intelligence AS a service

### Founder Credibility Plays
- **Warner**: "I was CTO of GitHub — the world's largest developer platform"
- **Kant**: "I built source{d} — we've been applying AI to code for years"
- **Team**: "We hired the VP of Global Sales from GitHub"
- **Investors**: "Nvidia invested $1B+ — they bet on infrastructure"
