# Poolside AI — Social Media & Content Activity

## Twitter/X Presence

### Official Account
- **Handle**: @poolaboratories (LinkedIn, not heavily active on X)
- **Strategy**: Poolside maintains a relatively LOW public social media profile
- **Primary community engagement happens in private Slack** (500+ curated AI builders, founders, creators)
- **Philosophy**: "No noise, just signal" — they deliberately avoid broad public broadcasting

### Founder Accounts
- **Jason Warner** (@jasondwarner): Active on X, posts about AI, coding, enterprise strategy. Uses platform for thought leadership and provocative takes (e.g., "most companies should NOT build foundation models")
- **Eiso Kant** (@eisokant): Active on X, more technical posts about model training, infrastructure, research

### Content Themes on Social Media
1. **Funding announcements** — major moments (Series B, Nvidia investment)
2. **Partnership announcements** — AWS, CoreWeave
3. **Thought leadership** — AGI vision, software engineering as path to AGI
4. **Product hints** — rarely direct product marketing, more vision-casting
5. **Hiring/culture** — team growth, engineering culture

### Social Media Strategy Assessment
- **Deliberately understated**: Poolside doesn't try to win on social media
- **PR-driven, not content-driven**: News coverage from funding rounds does the work
- **Founder-centric**: Jason Warner and Eiso Kant ARE the brand on social
- **Enterprise-appropriate**: Their customers (DoD, banks, F500) don't buy from Twitter — they buy from relationships and RFPs

## Key Takeaways

1. **Poolside's social strategy is "stealth in public"** — they let funding rounds and partnerships do the talking
2. **Founders are the content engine** — personal brands drive awareness more than company accounts
3. **They target AI researchers and enterprise buyers**, not individual developers
4. **YouTube/podcasts > Twitter** for their go-to-market — long-form content for enterprise decision-makers
5. **No community building for individual devs** — stark contrast to Cursor's developer community approach
6. **Defense/government customers don't discover tools on social media** — so minimal social investment makes strategic sense
