# Signal Studio — Go-To-Market Playbook
**ForwardLane | Confidential | March 2026**

---

## Executive Summary

Signal Studio is ForwardLane's flagship product: a signal-based GTM intelligence platform purpose-built for financial services. It combines natural language data querying, multi-provider enrichment, and real-time signal detection to tell enterprise asset managers, wealth management firms, and insurance distributors exactly **who to contact, when, and why** — before their competitors do.

This playbook governs all GTM activity for Signal Studio in 2026. It is the operating manual for revenue, not a strategy doc.

## 1. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### 1.1 Primary ICP: Enterprise Asset Management

**Who They Are**
- AUM: $50B+ (Tier 1 focus), $10B–$50B (Tier 2)
- Firms: BlackRock, Vanguard, Fidelity, Invesco, T. Rowe Price, Franklin Templeton, PIMCO, Capital Group, Putnam, Nuveen
- Key buyer: Chief Distribution Officer, Head of Advisor Sales, Head of Digital Distribution, VP of Institutional Sales
- Key influencer: Head of Marketing Technology, CTO/CIO, Sales Operations

**Pain Points Signal Studio Directly Solves**
- Wholesalers are flying blind — no signal of when advisors are actually ready to buy
- Territory management is static; allocations don't reflect real advisor intent
- New product launches rely on mass distribution lists, not ranked advisor likelihood-to-purchase
- Competitive displacement is reactive — they learn about it from the advisor, not from data
- Advisor attrition signals (retirement, M&A, moving to RIA) are caught too late

**What "Good" Looks Like**
- 50,000–150,000 advisors in their distribution universe
- Active Salesforce or Microsoft Dynamics deployment
- 25+ external wholesalers or internal sales team managing advisor relationships
- Budget authority in distribution tech or sales enablement ($250K+ annual spend)

**Trigger Events to Watch**
- New CDO or Head of Distribution hire (first 100 days budget allocation)
- Failed product launch (distribution didn't reach right advisors)
- Losing shelf space to a competitor
- RFP for data enrichment, advisor intelligence, or distribution technology

## 2. Signal Studio — Product Definition (GTM-Facing)

### Core Value Proposition
> "Signal Studio tells your wholesalers and sales teams exactly which advisors to call this week — and what to say — before anyone else does."

### What It Does (Features as Benefits)
| Feature | What It Does | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| Natural Language Query Engine | Query 50M+ advisor records in plain English | No SQL required — any wholesaler can use it |
| Multi-Signal Detection | Monitors 40+ behavioral, firmographic, and event signals | Replaces 5-7 point tools with one platform |
| Signal Scoring | Proprietary algorithm scores each contact 0-100 | Prioritizes outreach, eliminates noise |
| Compound Signal Alerts | Flags accounts with 2+ simultaneous signals | Identifies highest-urgency opportunities |
| Waterfall Enrichment | 7-provider data cascade: Hunter→FindyMail→Icypeas→QuickEnrich→Forager→Wiza→LeadIQ | Best contact data available, not just one provider |
| CRM Integration | Native Salesforce sync — signals appear on contact records | Zero workflow change for sales team |
| Action Playbooks | Pre-written signal-triggered outreach templates | Reps know what to say, not just who to call |
| Signal Attribution Dashboard | Tracks meetings, pipeline, and revenue back to signals | Proves ROI to CFO |

### Signals We Detect (Financial Services-Specific)
**Advisor-Level Signals**
- Advisor changes firms (within 7 days — while still in data systems)
- Advisor succession planning (mentions in business filings, state insurance filings, age cohort analysis)
- New advisor joins breakaway RIA
- Advisor adds new license/credential (CFP, CIMA, CPWA)
- Advisor opens new office location
- Advisor's firm gets acquired

**Account-Level Signals**
- Firm announces technology platform transition (new CRM, new portfolio management system)
- Firm hires new decision-maker (CEO, President, CTO — first 100 days)
- Firm experiences regulatory event (FINRA/SEC action — either threat or resolution)
- Firm announces M&A activity
- Firm launches new practice area (estate planning, ESG, alternatives)
- Competitor wholesaler territory reassignment (detected via LinkedIn job changes)

**Market Signals**
- Interest rate change cycle shifts (rate-sensitive product triggers)
- New ETF/fund regulatory filing detected (competitive product tracking)
- Industry conference attendance (scrape registration/speaker lists)
- Retirement product regulatory changes (SECURE 2.0 follow-on, etc.)

## 3. Pricing Strategy

### Pricing Philosophy
Signal Studio uses a **platform + usage hybrid model** — a base platform fee creates predictable revenue while usage-based components align pricing to value delivered. Pricing is anchored to business outcomes (advisor contacts reached, signals acted on) rather than seats alone.

### Tier 1: Enterprise — $180,000–$350,000 ACV
**Target:** Asset managers with $50B+ AUM, wirehouses
- Platform access fee: $10,000/month ($120K/year)
- Signal credits: 500,000 signals/year included
- Seats: Unlimited (all wholesalers)
- CRM integration: Salesforce native, full bi-directional sync
- Onboarding: Dedicated success manager, 90-day onboarding
- SLA: 99.9% uptime, <4 hour support response
- Overages: $0.30/signal above included volume
- Custom signals: Available for additional fee ($2,500/signal type/month)

**Why This Price:** At $180K ACV, Signal Studio costs less than one senior wholesaler. If it improves one wholesaler's meeting-set rate from 2% to 4%, the ROI is 10x in year one.

### Tier 2: Growth — $60,000–$120,000 ACV
**Target:** $10B–$50B AUM asset managers, RIA aggregators
- Platform access fee: $4,000/month ($48K/year)
- Signal credits: 100,000 signals/year included
- Seats: Up to 25 users
- CRM integration: Salesforce or HubSpot
- Onboarding: Group onboarding + documentation
- Overages: $0.40/signal above included volume

### Tier 3: Pilot — $24,000 (3-month pilot)
**Target:** Prospect conversion mechanism
- $8,000/month for 90 days
- 25,000 signal credits
- 5 seats
- Single signal type + CRM integration
- Converts to Tier 1 or Tier 2 at end of pilot
- Pilot fee credited toward first year ACV

### Professional Services (Add-On)
- Custom signal development: $5,000–$15,000 per signal type
- CRM workflow build-out: $10,000–$25,000 one-time
- Training and enablement: $2,500/day
- Data audit and cleanup: Scoped per engagement

### Pricing Psychology
- Lead with outcomes, not features: "500,000 advisor signals" → "enough signals to cover your entire distribution universe, every quarter"
- Anchor against headcount cost: One wholesaler costs $150K–$250K/year in salary + T&E. Signal Studio costs less and covers the whole team.
- Pilot de-risks the buy: 90-day pilot at $24K eliminates "we need to prove ROI" objection
- Never discount platform fee; offer extra signal credits instead

## 5. Sales Process

### Stage 1: Signal Detection → Initial Outreach (Day 0–3)

**Trigger → Action Protocol**
| Signal | Score | Action | Owner |
|--------|-------|--------|-------|
| New CDO/Head of Distribution hire | 8 | Personalized email from Nathan | Nathan/AE |
| Firm M&A announcement | 7 | AE outreach within 48h | AE |
| Competitor tech removal | 7 | SDR outreach with case study | SDR→AE |
| Funding event (PE-backed firm) | 6 | AE outreach within 1 week | AE |
| Conference attendance (same event) | 4 | Day-of LinkedIn + email | AE |

**Initial Email Standard**
- 3 sentences max
- Reference the specific signal (without being creepy): "I saw you joined [Firm] last month..."
- One clear value statement tied to their context
- One specific ask: 20-minute call, not "would love to connect"
- No attachments, no case study links in first email

---

### Stage 2: Discovery Call (Week 1–2)

**Call Objectives**
1. Confirm buying signal (validate our intelligence)
2. Understand current state: How do wholesalers prioritize their advisor lists today?
3. Quantify the pain: How many advisors in their universe? What's current meeting-set rate? What's a meeting worth?
4. Identify all stakeholders: Who else needs to be in the room?
5. Get commitment for demo

**Discovery Questions**
- "Walk me through how your wholesalers decide who to call on Monday morning."
- "When an advisor changes firms, how long before your team knows? How long before they act?"
- "If your team could contact the right 10 advisors every day instead of the 50 they currently have to sort through, what would that be worth?"
- "Who else cares about advisor coverage efficiency at your firm?"

**Qualification (MEDDIC)**
- **M**etrics: Meeting-set rate improvement, pipeline per wholesaler, cost-per-contact
- **E**conomic Buyer: CDO, CRO, or CSO — not sales ops or technology
- **D**ecision Criteria: Must be doing a data/technology evaluation, not pure relationship
- **D**ecision Process: Who signs? What's the approval chain? What's the budget cycle?
- **I**dentify Pain: Quantified pain, not vague dissatisfaction
- **C**hampion: Do we have an internal advocate?

---

### Stage 3: Demo (Week 2–4)

**Demo Structure (45 minutes)**
1. Recap discoveries (5 min): "Based on our call, here's what I understood..."
2. The "aha" moment — live query (10 min): "Let me show you your advisor universe right now." Pull their actual data through the platform.
3. Signal walkthrough (15 min): Show 3 signal types most relevant to their pain
4. Compound signal story (5 min): Show an account with multiple signals stacking
5. CRM integration (5 min): Show Salesforce record with signal data already populated
6. ROI calculator (5 min): Fill in their numbers live — meeting rate improvement × ACV × team size
7. Next steps (5 min): Propose pilot

**Demo Prep Requirements**
- Pull top 100 advisors in their territory from our database before the call
- Research their current product lineup and recent launches
- Find 2–3 "live" signal examples from their advisor universe to show
- Personalized ROI calculator pre-populated with their firm size

---

### Stage 4: Pilot (Month 1–3)

**Pilot Design**
- Duration: 90 days

**Pilot Management**

- Signal performance report every 2 weeks
- Mid-pilot executive review at day 45
- Pilot success review at day 80 (before day 90 — plant contract conversation early)

## 6. Competitive Positioning

### vs. Traditional BI Tools (Tableau, Power BI, Domo)

**Their Weakness**
These tools require a data team to configure, SQL skills to query, and weeks of setup to produce any insight. They're rearview-mirror reporting, not forward-looking signals.

**Our Narrative**
"You can build a dashboard that tells you what happened last quarter. Signal Studio tells you what's going to happen next week — and puts it directly in your wholesaler's Salesforce queue without any data team involvement."

**Trap Questions**
- "How long did it take your team to build your current reporting? How long does it take to change a report?"
- "Can your current system tell you when an advisor changes firms within 24 hours?"
- "Do your wholesalers actually use the dashboards, or do they check them once a quarter?"

---

### vs. Polsia / AI Automation Platforms (Bolt.new, similar)

**Their Weakness**
Polsia and Bolt.new-style platforms are built for indie hackers and digital nomads — they have zero financial services-specific data, no compliance awareness, no FINRA/SEC considerations, and no integration with the advisor data ecosystem (Broadridge, Morningstar, DST).

**Our Narrative**
"General-purpose AI automation tools don't know the difference between an RIA and a broker-dealer, can't access BrokerCheck data, and have never built a Salesforce Financial Services Cloud integration. Signal Studio was built for financial services from day one — every signal type, every integration, every workflow was designed for how distribution actually works."

**Trap Questions**
- "Does that platform have pre-built connections to your advisor data providers?"
- "How does it handle FINRA regulations on advisor communication tracking?"
- "Has their team ever worked with a compliance officer? Ours has."

---

### vs. ZoomInfo / Bombora / Generic Intent Platforms

**Their Weakness**
Generic intent data platforms show company-level web activity — not advisor-level behavioral signals. They can tell you "[Firm X] visited competitor websites" but they cannot tell you which advisor at [Firm X] is opening a new office, changing firms, or approaching retirement.

**Our Narrative**
"ZoomInfo tells you that a company is 'in-market.' Signal Studio tells you that Jane Smith, CFP at Merrill in Atlanta, just moved her book to an independent RIA and is now actively evaluating new product relationships — and here's her mobile number. That's the difference between category-level intelligence and actionable advisor intelligence."

**Trap Questions**
- "How advisor-specific is the intent data you're seeing? Is it account-level or contact-level?"
- "Can your current platform detect when a specific advisor changes firms within 72 hours?"
- "How does that platform handle the financial services advisor universe specifically?"

---

### vs. Existing CRM + Manual Research

**Their Weakness**
Salesforce is a database of what happened. It requires manual input, has stale data, and tells you nothing about what signals exist outside your current relationship. Manual research is not scalable — one wholesaler cannot monitor 3,000 advisors weekly.

**Our Narrative**
"Your Salesforce is a record of the past. Signal Studio is your intelligence about the future. We don't replace Salesforce — we plug directly into it and make every record smarter. Your wholesalers see the exact same workflow; they just get alerted when the right signals appear instead of guessing."
