## 🎯 The 6 Critical Objections

### 1. "Why not just use Salesforce reports?"

**Acknowledge:** "Salesforce reports are great for historical data and pipeline tracking — you've built a solid foundation there."

**Reframe:** "What we're adding is **forward-looking intelligence** that lives right inside Salesforce. Reports tell you what happened. This tells you what to say in tomorrow's meeting."

**Evidence:** "Reports don't write your talking points. They don't surface that Dr. Chen just increased her charitable giving by 40% and might be interested in donor-advised funds. That's meeting intelligence, not reporting."

**Demo Pivot:** "Let me show you the difference — look at what Brian would see when he opens Dr. Chen's record in Salesforce..."

---

### 2. "We already have YCharts/Morningstar"

**Acknowledge:** "YCharts and Morningstar are industry-standard research tools — your advisors rely on them for market data and analysis."

**Reframe:** "This isn't replacing research tools — it's **connecting the dots between research and conversations**. YCharts tells you the market moved. This tells you which clients to call about it and exactly what to say."

**Evidence:** "YCharts doesn't know that Mr. Thompson mentioned his daughter's college tuition in your last meeting, or that his risk tolerance shifted after his promotion. Our signals combine market data WITH relationship data to create personalized talking points."

**Demo Pivot:** "Look at this signal for Dr. Chen — it's pulling from market data but making it personal to her specific situation..."

---

### 3. "Our advisors won't adopt new tools"

**Acknowledge:** "Adoption is always the biggest challenge with new technology — I get why that's a concern."

**Reframe:** "That's exactly why we built this **inside Salesforce**. Brian doesn't learn a new tool — he just opens Salesforce like he does every morning. Zero training required."

**Evidence:** "The biggest adoption barrier is asking advisors to change their workflow. We're enhancing their existing workflow, not replacing it. It's meeting prep briefs where they already live."

**Demo Pivot:** "Let me show you what I mean — this is literally the same Salesforce view Brian uses today, just with better intelligence..."

---

### 4. "What about data security?"

**Acknowledge:** "Data security is non-negotiable in our industry — I completely understand the scrutiny."

**Reframe:** "Nothing leaves your Salesforce/Snowflake perimeter. We process data where it already lives, using your existing security controls."

**Evidence:** "We have architecture documentation showing exactly what touches your data. Most firms have us reviewed and approved by IT in under 2 weeks. We can provide a security brief for your team."

**Demo Pivot:** "The system you're seeing runs entirely within your data environment — let me walk through the technical architecture after the demo, but first let me show you the user experience..."

---

### 5. "How is this different from what we had before?"

**Acknowledge:** "You've invested in good systems — I want to make sure this adds real value, not just more noise."

**Reframe:** "Previous systems focused on **data aggregation**. This focuses on **conversation preparation**. Same data, but processed into actionable talking points that make every client interaction more valuable."

**Evidence:** "Instead of dashboards that tell you everything, you get morning briefs that tell you what matters today for each client. It's AI-powered meeting prep, not another analytics tool."

**Demo Pivot:** "Let me show you the difference — this isn't a dashboard, it's a morning brief. See how specific and actionable these talking points are..."

---

### 6. "Can we customize the signals?"

**Acknowledge:** "Absolutely — every firm has different priorities and relationship styles."

**Reframe:** "The **signal framework is fully configurable**. You define what matters to your advisors, and the AI learns your firm's conversation style and priorities."

**Evidence:** "We start with proven signal types that work across wealth management, then customize based on your specific book, client segments, and advisor preferences. This isn't one-size-fits-all."

**Demo Pivot:** "What you're seeing today are default signals, but let me show you the customization interface where you'd define your firm's specific priorities..."

## 🎭 Objection Handling Framework

For each objection, follow this 4-step structure:

1. **ACKNOWLEDGE** — Validate their concern (they feel heard)
2. **REFRAME** — Shift perspective to your value prop 
3. **EVIDENCE** — Provide specific proof/logic
4. **DEMO PIVOT** — Transition back to showing the product

## 🗣️ Key Phrases to Weave In

| Use These | When |
|---|---|
| **"Meeting prep briefs"** | Explaining what signals actually are |
| **"Zero learning curve"** | Addressing adoption concerns |
| **"Salesforce-embedded"** | Emphasizing integration |
| **"Morning briefs, not dashboards"** | Differentiating from past tools |
| **"Conversation intelligence"** | Positioning the core value |
| **"Easy buttons"** | Highlighting simplicity |

## 🚫 Avoid These Traps

- **Don't oversell** — Let the demo speak for itself
- **Don't get defensive** — Stay curious about their concerns  
- **Don't interrupt** — Let them finish their objection completely
- **Don't say "but"** — Use "and" or "that's exactly why..."
- **Don't go deep on tech** — Keep it business-focused unless they ask
