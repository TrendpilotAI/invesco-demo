## Mike's Exact Playbook (Detailed)

### Risk Minimization Framework
- **Every business is expected to succeed** — zero failures so far
- Find ideas that are **already working despite bad UX** → proven demand, just needs better execution
- This is the "can't fail" filter: if people pay for a crappy version, they'll pay more for a good one

### Team Structure (4 Co-Founders Model)
- Front-end developer
- Back-end developer  
- Designer
- Generalist/product person (Mike's role)
- **Always 4 co-founders, 25% each** → minimizes founder fallout (top startup killer)
- Everyone thinks about UX, not just the designer
- Tech-heavy team, lean on everything else

### Financial Model
- Grow to **$10K MRR first** → covers costs
- After $10K MRR → split profits between founders
- **Bigger salaries, NOT big exits** → lifestyle business by design
- Minimal reinvestment in ads or staff
- Stay super lean → maximum profit distribution

## Mike's 10-Step Playbook (Exact Steps)

1. **Pick an idea that's been done before** — new ideas are risky, need validation. Proven demand > novelty.
2. **Decide what's a good enough MVP** — study competitors, find what customers want most, ship fast.
3. **Offer a Lifetime Deal (LTD)** — $59-$100 one-time payment. Gets early users + capital.
4. **Never give away free accounts** — paying users actually use the product and give feedback.
5. **Hustle to sell private LTDs** — Reddit, Facebook groups, X, LTD communities. Frill raised $30K from private LTD alone.
6. **Start writing content IMMEDIATELY** — landing pages, blog posts, competitor pages, "alternative to" pages. Never too early. The longer it's indexed, the more traffic.
7. **Launch on AppSumo** — massive reach, huge email list. Target: close LTD phase with $100K in the bank for 1-2 years of content.
8. **One last private LTD** — FOMO play, raise prices slightly, close forever. Mail your list.
9. **Reviews are live-or-die** — Get LTD customers to write honest reviews on Trustpilot, G2. LTD community will help because they want you to succeed. Answer Reddit questions authentically.
10. **Transition to MRR** — Money depletes but MRR grows. By the time LTD capital runs out, MRR should sustain you.

### Ideas That "Can't Fail" vs. Ideas to Avoid
**Won't touch:** AI-focused businesses — too much platform risk (APIs you don't control)
