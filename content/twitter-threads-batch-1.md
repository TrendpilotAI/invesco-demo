## Thread 2: 3 things wealth mgmt firms get wrong about AI

**Tweet 1:**
Every wealth management firm says they're "AI-first" now. Most are AI-clueless. Here are the 3 biggest mistakes I see firms making with AI (and how to fix them) 🧵

**Tweet 2:**
Mistake #1: Thinking AI means chatbots. "We have ChatGPT integration!" No. Advisors don't need another search interface. They need intelligence that anticipates client needs. Chatbots make advisors work harder. Signals make advisors work smarter.

**Tweet 3:**
Mistake #2: Deploying AI on top of broken workflows. If your current process is manual chaos, adding AI just creates automated chaos. Fix the workflow first, then add intelligence. We've seen firms spend $2M on AI that automates the wrong things.

**Tweet 4:**
Mistake #3: Building for compliance, not advisors. "Our AI is 100% compliant and 0% useful." Start with advisor problems, then solve compliance. When we prioritized advisor workflows at ForwardLane, compliance actually got easier, not harder.

**Tweet 5:**
The fix: Start with one specific advisor pain point. Solve it completely. Ship it. Learn from real usage. Repeat. Don't try to boil the AI ocean. Pick one thing and make it 10x better than doing it manually.

**Tweet 6:**
Which of these mistakes is your firm making? The first step to AI success is admitting where you're stuck. What's the one advisor workflow you'd fix first?

## Thread 4: What happened when we gave advisors easy buttons instead of chatbots

**Tweet 1:**
"Just use the AI chatbot" is the "just Google it" of wealth management. Here's what happened when we gave advisors easy buttons instead of chat interfaces 🧵

**Tweet 2:**
Chatbots require advisors to know what to ask. Busy advisors don't have time to craft perfect prompts. They want: "Show me who to call this week" and "Tell me what to say." One click. Done.

**Tweet 3:**
We replaced our chatbot with action buttons: "Surface neglected clients," "Generate meeting agendas," "Flag allocation drifts." Same AI intelligence, zero prompt engineering required. Usage went from 23% to 89% in one quarter.

**Tweet 4:**
The insight: Advisors don't want to talk to AI. They want AI to do the thinking so they can focus on talking to clients. The best AI tools feel invisible — outcomes appear without cognitive overhead.

**Tweet 5:**
Example: Instead of "Ask AI about client portfolio performance," we built "Click here for underperforming clients this month." Same analysis, zero friction. The advisor gets a list of 5 clients to call with exactly what to discuss.

**Tweet 6:**
UI design matters more than AI capabilities. The smartest AI with bad UX loses to average AI with great UX every time. How would you design AI tools if advisors never had to think about the AI?
